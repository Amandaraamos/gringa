How Core Body Temperature May Influence Your Metabolism

SM

By Sarah Mitchell

Health & Wellness Writer • Updated: April 2026

A growing body of research is drawing attention to the relationship between internal body temperature and metabolic function. While the traditional focus of weight management has been on diet and exercise, scientists are now exploring whether thermoregulation plays a larger role than previously thought.

Studies published in reputable journals like Diabetes and Cell Metabolism have shown that brown adipose tissue (a type of body fat that generates heat) can be activated by cooler temperatures, leading to increased calorie expenditure. A study from the National Institutes of Health found that sleeping in a cool environment increased participants' brown fat activity and improved insulin sensitivity (Lee et al., 2014).

"Targeting thermoregulation may offer a complementary approach to supporting metabolic health," according to researchers at the NIH.

What Does This Mean for You?

While this research is still emerging, some practical strategies supported by the science include keeping your bedroom cool at night, incorporating brief cold exposure (like cool showers), and maintaining regular physical activity — all of which may support healthy metabolic function.
